False testimony The man who stabbed the victim recanted his testimony that Baker had killed the victim. The killer said he lied to get a shorter sentence for himself. The key evidence against McCracken was the testimony of a high school classmate, who recanted and said police bullied him into lying. A police officer paid an informant $500 to falsely accuse and testify against Carter. A judge ordered a new trial and prosecutors dismissed the charges. Three men told police Peterson had confessed to the crime while they were in a car together on their way to work and an inmate with pending charges said he heard Peterson say he killed the victim. Records showed Peterson did not work on the day coworkers said he confessed to them. The key witness against him, a convicted felon and crack cocaine addict, told Adams’ lawyer in 2007 she had turned her life around and confessed to lying at trial. She said police pressured her to testify against Adams. A jury acquitted him at a new trial. Three of the five witnesses from Wright’s 1993 trial who made statements that Wright had entered the victim’s house recanted their statements at the 2016 retrial. The other two witnesses had died.
